GUARANTEED LIVING BENEFIT RIDERS (Illustrative Example)

Guaranteed living benefit riders are optional features that policyholders
may add to a variable annuity contract, typically for an additional annual
fee, to provide income or withdrawal guarantees that are independent of
the actual investment performance of the contract.

Guaranteed Minimum Withdrawal Benefit (GMWB)

A GMWB guarantees that the policyholder may withdraw a specified
percentage of a "benefit base" each year for the rest of their life (or
for a set number of years, depending on the rider), even if the
underlying contract value is reduced to zero due to poor investment
performance or withdrawals.

The benefit base is generally separate from the actual contract value. It
is typically equal to purchase payments at issue and may increase over
time through:

- Roll-up: An annual guaranteed growth rate applied to the benefit base
  (commonly in the range of 5% to 7% annually) during years in which no
  withdrawals are taken, typically for a limited number of years (often
  10).

- Step-up (or ratchet): An automatic increase in the benefit base to match
  the contract value on a contract anniversary, if the contract value is
  higher than the current benefit base, typically reviewed annually.

The annual withdrawal percentage permitted under a GMWB is usually tied to
the policyholder's age at the time withdrawals begin, with older
policyholders generally permitted to withdraw a higher percentage (for
example, 4% at age 60 versus 5.5% at age 70).

Guaranteed Minimum Income Benefit (GMIB)

A GMIB guarantees a minimum level of annuity income if the policyholder
annuitizes the contract, regardless of actual account performance. Unlike
a GMWB, a GMIB generally requires the policyholder to convert the contract
value into a stream of guaranteed payments (annuitize) in order to access
the guarantee, rather than allowing ongoing partial withdrawals.

GMIB riders typically include a roll-up rate applied to a separate income
base, and a required minimum holding period (commonly 10 years) before the
guarantee can be exercised.

Guaranteed Minimum Accumulation Benefit (GMAB)

A GMAB guarantees that the contract value will be at least equal to a
specified amount (often purchase payments) after a defined holding period
(commonly 10 years), regardless of investment performance, without
requiring annuitization.

Rider Fees and Considerations

Living benefit riders are not free. Annual rider fees are typically
expressed as a percentage of the benefit base (not the contract value) and
commonly range from 0.50% to 1.50% annually, depending on the specific
guarantee selected. Because the fee is often based on the benefit base
rather than the contract value, the actual dollar fee may not decline even
if the contract value declines, since the benefit base can be higher than
the contract value after a market downturn.

Policyholders should weigh the cost of optional riders against the
likelihood that the guarantee will provide meaningful value, since riders
that are never used because the underlying investment performance was
strong still incur charges each year.
