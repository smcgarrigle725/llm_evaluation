VARIABLE ANNUITY PRODUCT OVERVIEW (Illustrative Example)

A variable annuity is a contract between a policyholder and an insurance
company. The policyholder makes one or more purchase payments, and the
insurance company agrees to make periodic payments back to the policyholder
beginning either immediately or at a future date.

During the accumulation phase, purchase payments are allocated among a
selection of investment sub-accounts. The value of the contract fluctuates
based on the performance of the underlying investment options chosen by the
policyholder. Unlike a fixed annuity, a variable annuity does not guarantee
a specific rate of return during the accumulation phase.

Key contract features typically include:

1. Accumulation Phase: The period during which purchase payments are made
   and the contract value grows or declines based on investment performance.

2. Annuitization Phase: The period during which the insurance company makes
   periodic payments to the policyholder, based on the accumulated contract
   value, the policyholder's age, and the payout option selected.

3. Death Benefit: Most variable annuity contracts include a standard death
   benefit, which guarantees that a beneficiary will receive at least the
   amount of purchase payments made, reduced proportionally for any
   withdrawals, even if the contract value has declined due to poor
   investment performance.

4. Optional Riders: Policyholders may elect to add optional riders for an
   additional fee, such as a Guaranteed Minimum Income Benefit (GMIB) or a
   Guaranteed Minimum Withdrawal Benefit (GMWB). These riders provide
   additional guarantees beyond the standard contract terms but increase
   the overall cost of the product.

5. Free Look Period: Most states require a free look period, typically
   between 10 and 30 days depending on the state of issue, during which the
   policyholder may cancel the contract and receive a full refund of
   purchase payments, or in some cases the contract value if it differs
   from purchase payments.

Fees and Charges

Variable annuities typically involve several layers of fees:

- Mortality and Expense Risk (M&E) Charge: Compensates the insurer for the
  insurance guarantees provided under the contract, typically expressed as
  an annual percentage of contract value (commonly in the range of
  1.00% to 1.50%).

- Administrative Fee: Covers recordkeeping and administrative costs,
  either as a flat annual fee or a small percentage of contract value.

- Underlying Fund Expenses: Fees charged by the investment sub-accounts
  themselves, similar to mutual fund expense ratios.

- Rider Fees: Additional charges for optional benefits, typically
  expressed as a percentage of either contract value or a separate
  "benefit base" used to calculate guaranteed amounts.

- Surrender Charges: See the Surrender Charge Schedule document for
  details on withdrawal charges during the surrender charge period.

This document is an illustrative summary for training and demonstration
purposes and does not represent the terms of any actual insurance product.
